# IronVeil Security — Brand Guidelines

## Company Overview
IronVeil Security is a B2B cybersecurity company founded in 2019, headquartered in Denver, Colorado. We specialize in **cloud-native endpoint protection**, **identity threat detection and response (ITDR)**, and **managed detection & response (MDR)** for mid-market and upper-mid-market companies (250–3,000 employees) across financial services, healthcare, and professional services verticals.

We are a Series B company ($47M raised, led by Andreessen Horowitz) with 180 employees and approximately 420 active customers. Our primary competitor set includes SentinelOne, CrowdStrike Falcon Go, and Huntress.

## Brand Voice

### Core Personality
IronVeil's voice is **sharp, honest, and a little irreverent**. We've grown tired of cybersecurity vendors who sell fear and deliver complexity. We believe security should be explainable, operable, and — where possible — even satisfying to work with.

- **Sharp**: We say things directly. No hedging, no qualifications stacked on qualifications.
- **Honest**: We acknowledge when things are hard, when tradeoffs exist, and when we don't have all the answers.
- **Irreverent**: We're allowed to have a point of view. We poke at industry sacred cows. We do not take ourselves too seriously.

### Tone by Channel
| Channel | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Blog (technical) | Direct, expert peer | Write as if talking to a senior analyst, not a prospect |
| Blog (executive) | Blunt, insight-first | Cut to the so-what in the first paragraph |
| LinkedIn | Conversational, punchy | No carousels, no "here are 7 things" unless earned |
| Email nurture | Warm, specific | Reference real events, not generic scenarios |
| Sales one-pagers | Confident, evidence-first | Lead with outcomes, not feature lists |

### What We Sound Like
> "Identity is now the perimeter. Your firewall didn't get the memo."

> "Alert fatigue isn't a tool problem. It's an architecture problem disguised as a workflow problem."

> "We don't sell peace of mind. We sell fewer 2am phone calls."

### What We Do NOT Sound Like
- Fear-forward messaging: "Hackers are evolving. Are you ready?" — Never.
- Buzzword stacks: "AI-powered next-gen cloud-native zero-trust platform" — Never.
- Passive corporate voice: "IronVeil is committed to excellence in security outcomes" — Never.
- Fake urgency: "Now more than ever, your organization needs..." — Never.

## Key Messaging Pillars

### 1. See Everything, Chase Nothing
Our core product promise. IronVeil reduces alert volume while increasing detection fidelity. We correlate signals so analysts aren't chasing ghosts.

### 2. Identity Is the New Perimeter
We were ITDR-first before ITDR was a category. This is our thought leadership stake in the ground. All executive content should reinforce this framing.

### 3. MDR Without the Black Box
Our MDR service is fully transparent — customers see every action taken, every playbook executed, every escalation reason. This differentiates us from legacy MSSPs.

## Target Audiences

### Audience 1: Security Directors & CISOs (Mid-Market)
- Pain: Understaffed teams, board pressure, alert overload, tool sprawl
- Goal: Defensible security posture without hiring 10 more analysts
- What they read: Dark Reading, Krebs on Security, vendor briefings, peer Slack groups
- What they distrust: Vendor case studies, NPS scores, "AI-powered" anything

### Audience 2: SOC Analysts (Tier 2/3)
- Pain: Repetitive triage, poor tooling integrations, lack of context in alerts
- Goal: Get to real threats faster, stop wasting time on noise
- What they read: Reddit r/netsec, SANS blog, vendor technical blogs, MITRE ATT&CK updates
- What they distrust: Marketing content, vendor webinars, anything that oversimplifies

### Audience 3: IT Directors / vCISOs (Channel Partners)
- Pain: Managing security across multiple clients with limited staff
- Goal: A product they can actually explain and deploy without chaos
- What they read: MSP-focused media, vendor partner portals, word-of-mouth
- What they distrust: Complex licensing, black-box reporting, poor partner support

## Content Principles

1. Lead with tension, not solution. Open with the problem in a way that makes the reader feel it.
2. One sharp claim per piece. Don't try to prove five things. Prove one thing well.
3. Use real data or say nothing. If you don't have a statistic, make a specific argument instead. Never use vague data ("studies show...").
4. No more than one CTA per post. Don't end blog posts with a list of asks. Pick one.
5. Acknowledge the hard thing. If there's a tradeoff or a legitimate counter-argument, name it. Our audience respects intellectual honesty.

## Brand No-Fly List
These words and phrases are banned in all IronVeil content:

- robust / robustness
- cutting-edge
- next-generation / next-gen (unless quoting analyst language)
- leverage (as a verb)
- holistic
- synergy / synergistic
- paradigm
- best-of-breed
- actionable insights
- seamless
- game-changer / game-changing
- solution (standalone, as in "our solution")
- empower / empowering
