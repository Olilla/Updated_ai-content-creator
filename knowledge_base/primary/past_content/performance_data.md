# IronVeil Security — Content Performance Data (Last 12 Months)

## Overview
Data pulled from HubSpot, LinkedIn Analytics, and Google Search Console. Period: May 2024 – April 2025. Analysis conducted by IronVeil Growth team.

## Top Performing Blog Posts (by Pipeline Influenced)

### 1. "Your EDR Isn't Watching Your Identity Layer. Here's Why That's a Problem."
- Published: September 2024
- Format: Long-form technical blog (~2,100 words)
- Audience: Security Directors / CISOs

- Average time on page: 6m 42s
- Demo requests influenced (HubSpot attribution): 34
- LinkedIn reposts: 112
- Why it worked: Led with a gap most readers hadn't articulated yet. Used IBM COSI 2024 data. Ended with a single CTA (free identity gap assessment), not a demo request.

### 2. "We Analyzed 600 Identity Alerts. Here's What Most Vendors Miss."
- Published: November 2024
- Format: Data-driven technical blog (~1,800 words)
- Audience: SOC Analysts
- Organic sessions (90 days): 11,200
- Average time on page: 8m 14s
- Demo requests influenced: 19 (lower than #1, but higher quality — 7 became opportunities)
- LinkedIn reposts: 287
- Why it worked: Original data. Specific number in the headline. No product pitch until the final paragraph. Was shared heavily in security Slack communities.

### 3. "The MSSP Black Box Problem (And What Transparent MDR Actually Looks Like)"
- Published: January 2025
- Format: Opinion/thought leadership (~1,400 words)
- Audience: CISOs evaluating MDR
- Organic sessions (90 days): 5,900
- Average time on page: 5m 18s
- Demo requests influenced: 41 (highest of any post this period)
- LinkedIn reposts: 78
- Why it worked: Named a real frustration our sales team hears constantly. Used a direct comparison structure. Ended with a demo CTA that felt earned, not forced.

### LinkedIn Insights
- Posts over 150 words consistently underperform — our audience scrolls fast
- Posts that acknowledge a counterargument or hard truth get 2–3x the comments of posts that don't
- Tagging competitors directly spikes impressions but generates noise; use sparingly and only when we have data to back it up
- Best posting days: Tuesday and Thursday, 8–9am MT

## Content That Underperformed

### "IronVeil Achieves SOC 2 Type II Certification" (Press Release Style Blog)
- Sessions: 890
- Time on page: 1m 12s
- Pipeline influenced: 0
- Lesson: Compliance announcements without a customer angle or "so what" don't travel. Reframe as: "What SOC 2 Type II Actually Means for Your Security Team" next time.

### "Top 10 Cybersecurity Trends for 2025"
- Sessions: 2,100
- Time on page: 2m 04s
- Pipeline influenced: 3
- Lesson: List posts with generic trends are forgettable. Readers bounce quickly. Our audience has seen this format 400 times. If we do a trends piece, it needs a specific, arguable thesis.

### Webinar: "Zero Trust for Mid-Market Companies"
- Registrants: 312
- Attendees (live): 89 (28% attendance rate — below industry avg of 35%)
- Pipeline influenced: 8
- Lesson: Webinar fatigue is real. On-demand recording got 201 additional views over 60 days. Consider replacing live webinars with short-form video + written companion piece.

## Audience Engagement Patterns

### What resonates most with CISOs
- Content that validates their existing frustrations before offering a solution
- Specific numbers and data (not vague percentages)
- Peer comparison framing ("here's what teams like yours are doing")
- Short executive summaries at the top of long pieces

### What resonates most with SOC Analysts
- Technical specificity (MITRE ATT&CK references, log sources named, query examples)
- Original data or analysis they can cite internally
- Acknowledgment that their current tools have real limitations
- No marketing language — they will stop reading immediately

### What resonates most with IT Directors / vCISOs
- Implementation details and realistic deployment timelines
- Partner-specific language (margins, co-managed models, white-label options)
- Content that helps them explain our product to their clients

## Content Gaps Identified (Internal)

1. We have no published content specifically targeting healthcare CISOs — a vertical that represents 28% of our customer base.
2. We have zero technical content on Active Directory-specific identity threats, despite AD being present in 55% of our customer environments.
3. Our competitor comparison content is outdated — last updated Q2 2024. CrowdStrike and SentinelOne have both released new tiers since then.
4. No content addresses the vCISO / MSP buyer specifically, despite 18% of our new ARR coming from channel partners.
