## Your EDR Isn’t Watching Your Identity Layer: Here’s Why It Matters

Many organizations believe that deploying an endpoint detection and response (EDR) solution is sufficient to fend off cyber threats. This mindset can be dangerously misleading. With **68% of breaches involving human elements** such as phishing and credential theft (Verizon DBIR 2024), it’s evident that focusing solely on endpoint security overlooks a critical vulnerability: your identity layer. When was the last time you assessed whether your identity security measures are keeping pace with evolving threats?

### The Shift in Attack Vectors

Credential-based attacks have eclipsed traditional vulnerabilities as the primary access vector for breaches. Research indicates that **credential theft and phishing attacks are at an all-time high**. In 2024, they accounted for a staggering portion of successful breaches. This shift is not just a statistical anomaly; it reflects a broader trend where attackers exploit the weakest link in the security chain — human behavior.

Most of us think of attacks in terms of malware or network exploits, but consider this: if your employees are the ones unwittingly providing access to attackers through compromised credentials, what good is your EDR? It’s like locking the front door of your house but leaving the windows wide open. The reality is that focusing solely on endpoint threats without addressing identity vulnerabilities is akin to ignoring a gaping hole in your defense.

### Limitations of EDR Tools

EDR solutions excel at detecting and responding to endpoint threats, but they often fall short in monitoring identity-related risks. For example, most EDR tools don’t track behaviors that signal credential abuse, such as impossible travel — where a user is logged in from two locations almost simultaneously. These gaps can have dire consequences. The **IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report 2024** estimates that the average cost of a data breach has reached **$4.88 million**. When you consider the potential fallout of undetected identity threats, that price tag becomes even more alarming.

Moreover, as organizations increasingly adopt cloud-based services, the perimeter has shifted from network borders to identity management. If your EDR isn’t watching this new perimeter, you're effectively blind to a significant portion of the threat landscape. It’s like having a security guard who only patrols the parking lot while ignoring the entry points of the building itself.

### The Rise of ITDR Solutions

The emergence of Identity Threat Detection and Response (ITDR) solutions underscores the importance of comprehensive security strategies that include identity monitoring. The ITDR market is projected to grow from **$1.2 billion in 2024 to $4.1 billion by 2028** (IDC), signaling a recognition among organizations that identity threats require dedicated attention and resources. 

ITDR solutions are designed to monitor identity activity continuously, detecting anomalies and potential threats that traditional EDR tools miss. For instance, they can flag unusual sign-in patterns or multiple failed authentication attempts — indicators of an impending credential compromise. By adopting ITDR, organizations can fill the gaps left by EDR and enhance their overall security posture.

### Addressing MFA Fatigue and Other Challenges

With the rise of **MFA fatigue attacks**, where attackers bombard users with multi-factor authentication requests until they relent and approve one, relying solely on MFA is no longer sufficient. In 2024 alone, **MFA fatigue attacks increased by 300%** (Okta Security Report). It’s a clear indication that attackers are evolving, and so must we.

Organizations must look beyond basic MFA implementations and consider integrating identity monitoring into their security frameworks. This is where an ITDR solution can provide real-time insights and alerts about suspicious activities, enabling teams to respond proactively rather than reactively.

The challenge lies in recognizing that the identity layer is now the frontline of cybersecurity. The conventional wisdom that EDR tools alone can secure your organization is increasingly outdated. 

### A Call to Action: Conduct an Identity Gap Assessment

As security leaders, it’s time we take a hard look at our current strategies. Have we fully integrated identity monitoring into our security frameworks? Are we prepared for the evolving threats that target our users and their credentials? Conducting an **identity gap assessment** can help identify vulnerabilities in your current security posture and point toward actionable insights to bolster your defenses. 

In a world where credential-based attacks are on the rise, recognizing the limitations of your existing EDR tools is crucial. The stakes are high, and the potential costs of inaction are even higher.
