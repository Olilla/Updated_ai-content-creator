## Building an EDR Strategy: What Mid-Market Companies Need to Know

You’re likely aware of the harsh realities surrounding data breaches. In 2024, the average cost of a breach reached **$4.88 million** (IBM), a staggering figure that underscores the financial stakes. Yet, many mid-market enterprises still focus primarily on endpoint security, often neglecting the critical identity layer that is increasingly exploited by attackers. Credential-based attacks have now eclipsed vulnerabilities as the leading access vector, with **68% of breaches involving a human element** (Verizon DBIR 2024). This shift necessitates a reevaluation of your Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) strategy.

### The Shift in Attack Vectors

Let's face it: your existing EDR solution may not be doing enough. Credential-based attacks are now the primary entry point for breaches, surpassing traditional vulnerabilities. This evolution is alarming and requires a dynamic response. Research suggests that **68% of breaches** involve human errors, such as phishing or credential mishandling. It's no longer just about protecting endpoints; it’s about safeguarding identities as well.

Many organizations mistakenly believe that a solid EDR solution alone can provide adequate protection. However, if you’re not monitoring identity behavior and authentication events, you’re missing a critical piece of the puzzle. In hybrid environments, where users access resources from various locations and devices, visibility must extend beyond endpoints to include the entire identity layer.

### Integration of EDR and ITDR

The line between EDR and Identity Threat Detection and Response (ITDR) is blurring, yet many EDR solutions still fail to address identity threats effectively. This oversight can lead to blind spots that attackers readily exploit. As organizations adopt more cloud services and remote workforces, the risks associated with identity threats have escalated.

Integrating EDR with ITDR capabilities can significantly enhance your threat detection and response. By monitoring identity activities—such as logins, privilege changes, and directory modifications—you gain a more comprehensive view of potential threats. The ITDR market is projected to grow from **$1.2 billion in 2024 to $4.1 billion by 2028** (IDC), indicating that organizations are acknowledging the importance of protecting the identity layer.

Imagine a bank that only monitors the vault but ignores the front door. That’s what relying solely on EDR for security looks like today. You need to keep an eye on who’s entering and exiting your environment, not just what’s happening inside it.

### Operationalizing EDR for Mid-Market Needs

Mid-market companies often grapple with resource constraints that larger enterprises don’t face. Your security team is likely smaller, and budgets are tighter, which makes it crucial to deploy EDR solutions that are not only effective but also easy to manage. The last thing you need is a complex system that requires extensive training or constant oversight.

Look for solutions with a lightweight agent and real-time telemetry capabilities. A lightweight agent should have a minimal CPU and memory footprint, allowing for quick deployment across your network without bogging down performance. To combat this, your EDR strategy must prioritize real-time detection and response. If your team is spending weeks identifying breaches or responding to incidents, your current EDR strategy may not be sufficient.
